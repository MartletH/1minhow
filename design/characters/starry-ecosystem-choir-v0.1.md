# Starry Ecosystem Choir — Character System v0.1

## Product intent

Eight named animal characters form the first IP-ready cast. Each has a public-domain painting gene, a natural role, a distinctive silhouette, and a repeatable behavior language. Supporting stars, moon, clouds, plants, mushrooms, water drops, insects, fish schools, and music marks remain secondary decorative elements.

The system is designed for small distributed accents similar to Google Doodle usage: hero-edge details, section labels, card corners, empty states, footer notes, loading states, and subtle motion. It is not a full-page crowded illustration.

## Allowed variation boundary

- Clothing, scarves, small bags, seasonal leaves, flowers and weather accessories may vary.
- Core body proportions, face shape, ear/tail/wing structure, primary palette and signature painting-gene marker do not vary.
- A new outfit cannot cover the character's main recognition point.
- A generated image that loses two or more fixed memory points is not the same production character and must not enter the canonical asset set.
- Any major redesign creates a new character asset version rather than silently overwriting v0.1.

Each character must receive at least these eight distinct states. Personality must be visible through body language, not only facial swaps.

## Relationship logic

- Mona Meow is a calm observing center, not a leader.
- Vincent Owl and Pearl Bun represent seeing and listening.
- Sunny Vulp and Luna Leo form a day/night pair.
- Monet Frog and Hoku share care for water rhythm.
- Millet grounds the cast in resource care and everyday ecological practice.
- The cast is a choir of different living modes, not a human job hierarchy.

## Supporting elements

Supporting elements do not receive equal IP prominence in v0.1: expressive stars, sleepy moon, clouds, meteors, mushrooms, leaves, vines, tiny flowers, water drops, fireflies, music marks, fish schools, and stones.

## Production requirements

- Establish silhouette sheet before detailed color.
- Create front, 3/4, side, back, and top-view references for animation consistency.
- Produce the eight-state action sheet for every main character.
- Validate readability at 32 px, 64 px, and 96 px.
- Export transparent SVG where appropriate and transparent PNG/WebP for textured art.
- Maintain one manifest entry per asset with character, state, source painting, prompt/source file, dimensions, license note, and SHA-256.
- Do not use protected modern artworks as source genes.
- Canonical visual sheets and approved exports must be committed with immutable hashes before public use.
